Frequently Asked Questions

What are the prerequisites for the 200-hour training?

We recommend a minimum of 6 months consistent yoga practice before enrolling in our 200-hour program. While experience with various styles is helpful, a genuine interest in deepening your practice and a commitment to the learning process are the most important prerequisites.

Is this training suitable if I don't want to teach yoga?

Absolutely! Many of our trainees join the program to deepen their personal practice and understanding of yoga, without the intention to teach. The training offers valuable tools for personal growth, regardless of your career aspirations.

What is the time commitment outside of training hours?

Students should expect to spend an additional 5-10 hours per week on homework, reading, practice teaching, and personal practice. This varies based on your learning style and familiarity with the material.

Do you offer scholarships or work-study opportunities?

Yes, we offer a limited number of partial scholarships and work-study positions for each training. Please indicate your interest in these options on your application, and we'll provide additional information during the interview process.

What if I miss a session?

While we encourage full attendance, we understand that life happens. You can miss up to 10% of the training hours and still complete the program by doing make-up work. Beyond that, additional fees may apply for private make-up sessions.

What styles of yoga does the training focus on?

Our 200-hour program provides a strong foundation in alignment-based vinyasa and hatha yoga, with exposure to yin, restorative, and meditation practices. The 300-hour program allows for more specialization in your areas of interest.
